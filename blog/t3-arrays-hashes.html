Arrays and hashes

What is the difference between arrays and hashes?

July 26, 2015

Arrays and Hashes are two ways of storing and accessing data. They allow you to collect an amount of keyword, numbers etc.

Arrays

Arrays are basically just lists. They can list objects like strings, integers, floats or other arrays. You can use arrays when you just want to store a list of items. Example: favorite_animals = [“turtles", “cats", “dogs", “birds"]

Hashes

Hashes are pretty similar to arrays. However, the difference is that hashes store two kinds of information: key and value. In this context the value defines the key by adding it a sort of definition. Example: personal_information={:last_name => "Buck", :first_name => "Katharina", :age => 100}
